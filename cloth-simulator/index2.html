Cloth Simulator Project

Exploring realistic fabric behavior through physics and advanced graphics techniques.

Project Overview

This project features a real-time cloth simulator that models how fabric moves under physical forces like gravity and collisions. Beyond realistic motion, it incorporates custom visual effects using GLSL shaders to enhance the fabric's appearance.

How It Works

The cloth is simulated as a grid of points (point masses) connected by springs, mimicking real fabric behavior. Three types of springs are used to replicate different forces:

  • Structural Springs: Connect neighboring points to resist stretching and compression.
  • Shearing Springs: Prevent diagonal distortion of the fabric.
  • Bending Springs: Resist folding, maintaining smoothness by connecting points two spaces apart.

This structure allows the cloth to respond naturally to movement and external forces.

Simulation Features

Physical Properties: The simulator lets users adjust parameters to see how the cloth behaves under different conditions. Key parameters include:

Parameter Effect
Spring Constant (ks) Controls stiffness. Low ks creates soft, droopy fabric with slow, large waves, taking longer to settle. High ks results in stiff fabric with small, fast vibrations, stabilizing quickly.
Density Affects weight. Higher density causes more drooping due to gravity, with slower movement. Lower density results in lighter fabric with less sagging.
Damping Reduces motion over time. High damping makes the cloth settle faster, while low damping leads to prolonged oscillations.

Visual Effects with Shaders

Shaders, written in GLSL, enhance the cloth's visual realism by running parallel computations on the GPU for faster rendering. Two main types of shaders are used:

  • Vertex Shaders: Transform the cloth's shape and position.
  • Fragment Shaders: Determine the color and lighting of each pixel after transformation.

Several shading techniques are implemented:

  • Blinn-Phong Shading: Combines ambient (uniform light), diffuse (directional light based on surface angle), and specular (highlight glare) components for realistic lighting.
  • Bump and Displacement Mapping: Add surface texture illusions. Bump mapping alters lighting to simulate texture, while displacement mapping physically alters geometry for a more pronounced effect.
  • Environment Mapping: Simulates reflections, ideal for shiny materials like silk.

Experimentation and Results

Adjusting simulation parameters reveals diverse cloth behaviors:

  • Softer or denser cloth shows deeper folds and slower stabilization.
  • Stiffer cloth retains shape with minimal deformation, settling quickly.
  • Collisions and self-interactions create realistic wrinkles, especially with low ks or high density, leading to more pronounced creases.

Shaders significantly enhance visual fidelity, making the fabric appear textured, reflective, or shiny based on the chosen effect. For instance, displacement mapping creates a coarser surface at higher resolutions compared to the smoother effect of bump mapping.

Summary

This cloth simulator blends physics-based animation with cutting-edge graphics to create interactive and visually compelling fabric simulations. Designed to be educational and engaging, it allows users of all backgrounds to explore the principles of cloth behavior and rendering techniques in computer graphics.
